# Ethics & Policy Statement — Customer Support Assistant

## Overview

This document outlines the ethical risks, mitigations, intended use, and 
limitations of the Customer Support Assistant system built for 6INTELSY 
(Intelligent Systems), 2nd Semester AY 2025-2026. This system is an academic 
prototype and is NOT intended for real-world deployment.

---

## Intended Use and Limitations

This system is designed solely for academic demonstration of NLP and 
Reinforcement Learning concepts applied to e-commerce customer support 
intent classification. It should not be used to:

- Make real decisions about customer orders, refunds, or accounts
- Replace human customer service agents in any production environment
- Handle sensitive personal, financial, or legal information

---

## Risk Register

### Risk 1 — Misclassification Leading to a Wrong Response
**Likelihood:** High | **Severity:** High

The model may confidently misclassify a customer message and return an 
irrelevant or misleading response. For example, a message about a billing 
dispute could be classified as a general complaint, resulting in a generic 
response that does not address the customer's actual concern.

**Mitigations:**
- A confidence threshold of 0.70 is applied. Any prediction below this 
  threshold is flagged rather than auto-responded to, and would be escalated 
  to a human agent in a real deployment scenario.
- Misclassified examples are logged and analyzed in the evaluation pipeline 
  to identify the most commonly confused intent pairs.
- The system is clearly labeled as a prototype with a disclaimer that 
  responses are not final or binding.

---

### Risk 2 — Unequal Performance Across User Groups
**Likelihood:** Medium | **Severity:** High

The training data is synthetically generated and may not represent the full 
diversity of real customer language. Users who write in informal English, 
use very short messages, or phrase things in non-standard ways may receive 
worse intent predictions than users who write in complete, formal sentences. 
This creates an unequal experience across user groups.

**Mitigations:**
- Slice analysis is performed during evaluation, comparing Macro-F1 scores 
  for short utterances (8 words or fewer) versus long utterances (more than 
  8 words).
- Per-class F1 scores are reported in the evaluation output to identify 
  which specific intents perform poorly.
- The class imbalance identified during EDA (cancel_order having 
  significantly fewer samples than other intents) is documented and 
  flagged for potential upsampling in the final training run.

---

### Risk 3 — RL Agent Bias Toward Frequent Intents
**Likelihood:** Medium | **Severity:** Medium

The RL agent learns which responses to select based on reward signals 
from a simulation over the test set. Because frequent intents appear 
more often in the data, the agent may over-optimize for common queries 
(e.g., track_order, complaint) and perform poorly on rare but important 
ones (e.g., cancel_order, track_refund).

**Mitigations:**
- Cumulative reward is tracked and reported separately for high-frequency 
  and low-frequency intent groups in the evaluation script.
- The epsilon-greedy exploration mechanism (ε = 0.10) ensures the agent 
  does not entirely stop exploring less common actions.
- A confidence threshold fallback prevents the agent from acting on 
  low-confidence predictions entirely, reducing the chance of confidently 
  wrong actions on rare intents.

---

## Privacy and Consent

- The dataset used (Bitext Customer Support Dataset) is synthetically 
  generated. It contains no real user data and no personally identifiable 
  information (PII).
- No real customer data was collected, stored, or used at any point in 
  this project.
- The system does not log user inputs from the GUI beyond the current 
  session.

---

## Fairness Checks Planned

- Per-class F1 reporting across all 27 intent categories
- Slice analysis by utterance length (short vs. long)
- Separate RL reward tracking for frequent vs. infrequent intents
- Confusion matrix analysis to identify systematic misclassification patterns

---

## Disclaimer

This system is an academic prototype built for educational purposes only. 
It is not validated for clinical, legal, financial, or any real-world 
customer-facing use. All responses generated by this system are based on 
pre-written templates and do not constitute official advice or decisions. 
Users of this prototype should not rely on its outputs for any real 
customer support decisions.
